Who I Am

Connecting mechanics,
electronics, and code.

I'm Simr Virk, a Mechatronic Systems Engineering student at Simon Fraser University, graduating in 2027. My discipline lives at the intersection of mechanical design, embedded electronics, and software — and I believe engineers who are fluent in all three build things that engineers who aren't, simply can't.

From writing firmware at Motorola Solutions for mission-critical public safety hardware, to designing a 5-DOF vision-guided robot arm from scratch, I gravitate toward problems where precision is non-negotiable and the margin for error is tight. That's where I do my best work.

Outside the technical work, I'm driven by a broader question: what makes a system elegant? Whether it's a gear train, a control loop, or a team — I'm always thinking about how the parts fit together. I'm actively seeking co-op and full-time roles in robotics, autonomous systems, or embedded hardware-software integration where I can build things that matter at scale.

07 — Gaming

Immersive
Worlds

I really enjoy games that demand mechanical skills, careful observation, or just genuine emotional investment in the world. I really appreciate games designed with intent, so in the slideshow below are some of my favourite games that I have played. I also rated my top 5 games in no particular order.

Souls-likes Single-Player RPGs Open World Action-Adventure
  • Elden Ring
    This was my first souls-game, and the experience was unforgettable. The insanely difficult bosses (Malenia took me 5 hours) got me more into single-player games, but the story also had me hooked. I love when a game has good lore (shoutout FNAF), and I spent hours watching YouTube videos on the story behind this universe. George R. R. Martin wrote this story, and I loved Game of Thrones and A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms, so it only made sense that I was hooked into this lore as well.
  • Super Mario Galaxy
    The best Wii game of all time, and no one can change my mind. Everything about this game was so peak, and I wish I could go back in time to when I was 10 years old to replay this game.
  • Red Dead Redemption 2
    The story Red Dead Redemption 2 gives is one that will always stick with me. I was attached to the Van der Linde gang (I love Lenny), and the missions I went on with them were insanely fun. The ending was super emotional, and the epilogue is severely underrated as well. Arthur Morgan will always be my GOAT.
  • Roblox
    This was the first video game I ever played, and I don't think I would have got into engineering if it wasn't for Roblox. I used to hop on call everyday with my best friend in Grade 5 to play Phantom Forces, Twisted Murderer, Apocolyse Rising, and other games.
  • Valorant
    Controversial pick, but Valorant got me back into gaming after I went on a long hiatus after Roblox. I only played ranked, and it was fun to watch myself get better at the game while also climbing the ranks. I peaked at 2 RR below Diamond 1 (smh).
08 — Anime & Manga

Illustrated
Stories

I only recently got into anime and manga, and I found that I gravitate toward stories with serious world-building and high-stakes narratives. I like the kind of stories that make you think about it long after you've finished it, so the slideshow below leans toward titles that either redefined the medium or made me genuinely reconsider something (they are all mainstream as I haven't got into the niche stuff yet). You can also see my top 5 favourite stories in no particular order.

Shonen Seinen World-Building High-Stakes Narrative
  • Attack on Titan
    My all time favourite anime, and it's not even close. It has most complete narrative in anime, and literally every detail is there for a reason. I know the finale was divisive, but the journey to get there was unmatched.
  • Vinland Saga
    I read the manga before the anime came out, and I loved the slow-burn deconstruction of revenge and violence. I believe Thorfinn's character arc from Season 1 to Season 2 is the best character growth in the medium, but that doesn't take away from the fact that Askeladd is my character.
  • Jujutsu Kaisen
    JJK is perfectly paced, thematically rich, and emotionally devastating. The fight scenes are so well animated, and I can't wait for Gojo vs Sukuna. The Sendai Colony episode was also one of the best animated episodes of all time.
  • Berserk (Manga)
    The Golden Age can't be matched by any other arc. The brutality of it is something I haven't seen before, and this was the story that got me into reading manga.
  • Hunter x Hunter
    After reading Berserk, I needed something a little more light-hearted and fun to consume and this was the story to do it. Gon and Killuas adventures were entertaining to watch, and I thought it would be like that the entire time until I hit the Chimera Ant arc. That caught me so off-guard, and Gon's transformation gave me chills.
