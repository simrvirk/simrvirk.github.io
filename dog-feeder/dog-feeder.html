Fully-automated gravity-fed dog feeder eliminating irregular meal scheduling. DS3231 RTC dispatches 3 timed daily meals via a servo-actuated metering arm; internal ultrasonic sensor monitors kibble inventory in real time — ±5g dispensing repeatability, zero manual intervention.

Hardware & Sensing
  • DS3231 RTC (I²C) — ±2 ppm timekeeping accuracy; coin-cell backup retains schedule through power loss
  • Servo-actuated metering arm — sweeps at scheduled time, holds calibrated dwell period to release a single portion, returns to closed position; ±5g dispensing repeatability
  • Ultrasonic sensor — samples fill level at 1 Hz; maps raw distance to volumetric fill % via pre-characterized hopper geometry curve; dual-LED array switches green→red below 10% capacity
  • Debounced push-button interrupt — 50 ms software debounce; toggles meal-skip flag suppressing the next dispense without modifying the RTC-stored schedule
  • Internal wiring routing — all cables dressed internally for a clean, consumer-grade exterior
Mechanical Design & Retrospective
  • Gravity-fed hopper — designed in SolidWorks for passive, jam-free kibble flow; hopper and top cover 3D-printed in PLA on Bambu P1S
  • v1 failure mode identified — kibble load induces torque-driven sag at servo arm tip, creating a gap between arm and hopper wall → intermittent jamming and inconsistent portions
  • v2 redesign — replace metering arm with DC motor-driven waterwheel disc (radial prongs spaced to single-portion volume, rotating over fixed aperture); eliminates cantilever loading and provides deterministic per-revolution portion control
  • Material oversight — PLA is not food-contact safe; v2 switches to food-grade PETG or ABS
  • Key lesson — early mechanism selection locks in downstream constraints that are expensive to fix retroactively; v1 servo arm choice constrained hopper geometry, bowl placement, and portion consistency simultaneously
