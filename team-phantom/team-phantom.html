Deep Dive

Inverter Mounting
System Design

  • Why it's complex: The inverter is the highest-current component on the car — its mounting position simultaneously governs coolant routing, HV cable bend radii, and structural load paths; a wrong decision cascades into rework across multiple sub-systems
  • Interface research first: Mapped connector locations, coolant inlet/outlet port geometry, service access faces, and accumulator cable routing with the electrical team before opening SolidWorks; FSAE tractive system isolation requirements layered on top
  • Iterative FEA design: Steel tube-and-gusset structure welded to spaceframe; each configuration validated with static FEA across 4 chassis attachment points; geometry refined to resolve stress concentrations and trim mass where simulation showed margin
  • Fabrication execution: Issued dimensioned drawings → tube cuts in SFU machine shop → BCIT water jet for gusset plates → coordinated chassis weld-up; installed with zero play at all attachment points
