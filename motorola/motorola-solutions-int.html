What I Built

Key Contributions

01 — Flagship · Term 2
Automated FOV Goniometer System
Replaced the DVT's manual flat-wall trigonometry FOV method with a fully automated system. A Zaber motorized rotary stage sweeps a green laser line while a Python/OpenCV pipeline monitors the DUT's live RTSP stream — FOV is measured directly via the motor's rotary encoder, making physical standoff distance irrelevant and eliminating the dominant error source. Achieved ±0.5° accuracy versus the Trioptics ImageMaster ($40K+ instrument) with 85% reduction in test cycle time.
02 — Computer Vision · Term 2
RTSP Pipeline & Single-Channel Thresholding
Built a real-time Python/OpenCV pipeline ingesting live RTSP streams from cameras under test. Initial RGB-channel processing failed at wide-angle edges — chromatic aberration caused color fringing that corrupted laser boundary detection. Resolved by extracting only the raw green grayscale channel with strict binary thresholding, yielding sharper, faster, aberration-immune edge detection.
03 — Architecture · Term 2
Discrete Step Engine (DSE)
Continuous velocity sweep commands caused asynchronous TCP buffer collisions from Zaber network latency, dropping the motor controller mid-sweep and corrupting angular readings. Replaced with the Discrete Step Engine: motor advances in fixed 0.05° increments, comes to a full stop, flushes the TCP buffer, then evaluates the current frame — guaranteeing every encoder position is synchronized to its corresponding video frame with no interpolation.
04 — Optical Metrology · Term 2
Advanced Error Correction Suite
Identified and corrected three independent systematic error sources before the ±0.5° target was achievable. Finite beam width: inflated raw FOV readings — resolved via automated pre-sweep calibration that dynamically profiles and subtracts beam angular width per session. Focus breathing: autofocus hunting in darkness caused frame-to-frame focal length drift — resolved with controlled ambient lighting and high-contrast reference targets. Entrance pupil parallax: rotating about the front lens instead of the nodal point introduced up to 2.2° geometric error — root cause identified; macro rail calibration procedure proposed for future elimination.
05 — Environmental Testing · Term 1
Camera Validation & Environmental Stress Testing
Performed Hi/Low Temperature, ALT, condensation cycling, and cold-start verification across multiple camera product lines in dedicated climate chambers. Conducted optical and image quality baselining (FOV, occlusion, vignetting) and lens performance assessments. Developed Python and Bash scripted automation to reduce manual burden on high-repetition procedures.
06 — Cross-Team Collaboration · Term 1
Failure Investigation, Root Cause Analysis & FAI
Performed failure investigations and RCA on pre-production hardware in collaboration with mechanical, electrical, and firmware teams — from issue reproduction through fix validation. Conducted First Article Inspections (FAI) on new production units and documented findings in structured test reports for escalation through engineering channels.
Deep Dive — Term 2

FOV Goniometer
Automation System

  • The problem: The DVT's standard FOV method used flat-wall trigonometry (FOV = 2 × arctan(BC / 2AD)) — a 1 cm distance error at 50 cm standoff introduced 1.3% FOV error; the method was geometrically invalid for fisheye and wide-angle lenses; and manual edge identification made repeatability impossible to certify
  • The architecture: Replaced linear distance measurement with direct angular encoder feedback — a Zaber motorized rotary stage sweeps a green laser line while the DUT observes from a fixed point on the rotation axis; Python records the encoder angle at the moment the laser reaches each frame edge; standoff distance becomes mathematically irrelevant, eliminating the dominant error source
  • Discrete Step Engine: Continuous velocity sweep caused asynchronous TCP buffer collisions from Zaber network latency, dropping the motor controller mid-sweep; replaced with DSE — motor advances 0.05° increments, stops completely, flushes the TCP buffer, then evaluates the current frame; every encoder angle guaranteed to match its corresponding video frame with no interpolation
  • Error correction suite: Three systematic sources required targeted solutions — chromatic aberration at wide-angle edges solved by switching to single green-channel binary thresholding; finite beam width solved via automated pre-sweep calibration per session; focus breathing in dark environments solved with controlled ambient lighting and high-contrast reference targets
  • Residual work — nodal point parallax: After all corrections, residual ~0.5° error traced to entrance pupil offset from the rotary stage axis; proposed macro rail nodal point calibration procedure for future elimination
