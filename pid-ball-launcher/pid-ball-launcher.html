Ball Launcher

Architected 100% of the mechanical design and closed-loop control system for an autonomous multi-ball sorting and launching platform — 1st place out of 20 teams, MSE 312. 30g FEA-optimized truss arm + modular MATLAB/Simulink PI architecture enabled full trajectory calibration in <90 minutes after a multi-day circuit fault was resolved on competition morning.

Mechanical Design
  • 177.35mm truss arm — 30g; FEA-optimized triangular cutouts positioned to minimize rotational inertia (38,687 g·mm²); 0.63mm simulated deflection under 10N tip load
  • Ball holder accepts all three projectile diameters up to ø27mm — cedar (ø20mm), glass (ø12mm), fluffy yellow (ø25mm)
  • Bifilar pendulum test validated inertia estimate; measured oscillation period of 1.25s
  • FEA vs. experiment discrepancy — 5N load yielded 4.5mm measured deflection vs. 0.63mm simulated; traced to compliance at the 3D-printed square-shaft attachment interface, not the truss structure itself
  • Ball-sorting mechanism designed from scratch — sensors and actuators selected to autonomously classify all three projectile types prior to the throw sequence; six throws executed in random order with no human intervention
Closed-Loop Controls & Competition
  • PI control in MATLAB/Simulink — motor encoder feedback; H-bridge driver via Arduino PWM; P = 0.65, I = 0.35, D = 0; settles in 3–4 oscillations with ~30% overshoot
  • D-term excluded — derivative gain introduced severe motor vibration from high-frequency PWM switching noise; lead compensator also tested and rejected for same reason
  • Modular angle-per-ball architecture — each ball type controlled by a single angle parameter, seeded from kinematic launch angle calculations; designed specifically so calibration could be done rapidly from a known starting point
  • Circuit fault root cause — multi-day failure traced to a faulty terminal block causing inconsistent power delivery to the motor driver; fix was to eliminate the terminal block and hard-wire the supply directly
  • Competition morning — hardware operational for the first time ~90 min before competition; all launch trajectories for both ball types calibrated and locked before we went up
  • Result: 1st / 20 teams — full score on cedar vessel placement and fluffy yellow distance targeting
